## **2\) Executive Summary (dense, factual)**

**What changed (science \+ engineering):**

* **Science:** The program moved from broad sweeps (**16Y, 16Z**) into targeted microscope tests that isolated:  
  (a) a **damp≈20 anomaly regime** with high anomaly/marker incidence (**16AH, 16AI-A**),  
  (b) an **order/precedence dependence** regime (**16AK, 16AL**),  
  (c) **history sensitivity** that collapses under a “hard baseline reset” control (**16AO vs 16AP**), and  
  (d) a **sharp threshold ridge** with **measurable hysteresis** under certain sweep protocols (**16AX\_v2, 16AY, 16AZ, 16BA**).  
* **Engineering:** Baseline tooling advanced from **SOLBaseline\_v1.3.js → SOLBaseline\_v1.5.js**, adding snapshot/restore of node kinematics and select physics globals (e.g., `globalBias`, `_t` if present), enabling tighter anti-drift discipline in history tests.

**What was proven/strengthened vs still exploratory:**

* **Strengthened (Supported):** The “sharp ridge” characterization (probabilities jump sharply over small `multB` bands; see 16AR/16AV/16AW/16AY).  
* **Exploratory (Provisional):** The *extent* and *mechanism* of hysteresis/history dependence; it is clearly present in some protocols and minimal/absent in others (compare 16AY vs 16BA).

**Top 5 outcomes:**

* **(1)** **damp=20** shows a high anomaly \+ marker rate in an anomaly hunt (92% in 16AH at damp 20).  
* **(2)** GateMap shows **marker onset and timing concentrating at tick≈8** at high damping (16AI-A).  
* **(3)** BusSteal shows **114 activity without arbiter ownership** under that probe (16AI-B).  
* **(4)** History sensitivity exists under one baseline mode (16AO) but **vanishes under hard reset** (16AP).  
* **(5)** Threshold ridge is **quantified** with bracket estimates and hysteresis widths (e.g., 16AW peak threshold ≈0.942; 16AY peak thresholds differ up vs down).

**Anomalies / damp regime**

  * `packetClass=136_then_114_fast`  
  * `precedence=136_first`  
  * Source: `sol_phase311_16ah_anomaly_rows.csv`.

**Gate/marker timing**

* 16AI-A gate map shows marker rates rise strongly at higher damping:  
  * `marker_present_rate`: damp 18 → 0.28, damp 20 → 0.90, damp 22 → 0.92  
  * First marker timing concentrates early: at damp 20 and 22, `tick=8` is the most common first marker tick.  
  * Sources: `sol_phase311_16ai_A_gateMap_byDamp_summary.csv`, `sol_phase311_16ai_A_gateMap_firstMarkerTick_dist.csv`.

**Bus “steal” probe**

**Order dependence \+ lane identity shifts**

* 16AK shows that at `multBUsed >= 1.44`, `prec114=40/40` and `arb114=40/40` for both damp 12 and damp 20 in grouped results.  
  * Source: `sol_phase311_16ak_group_by_damp_multB.csv`.  
* 16AK tick8 max1 distribution shifts:  
  * At damp12, multB=1.144: tick8 max1 is `90->92` in 40/40 runs.  
  * At damp12, multB=1.44+: tick8 max1 becomes `95->114` in 38–40/40 runs.  
  * At damp20, multB=1.144: mostly `95->114` (35/40), with some `136->89` and rare `114->122`.  
  * Source: `sol_phase311_16ak_lane_tick8_counts.csv`.

  * Source: `sol_phase311_16al_T2_group_by_damp_multB.csv`.

**History sensitivity (baseline/initialization dependence)**

* 16AO shows arbiter ownership rates vary by history block:  
  * A\_pre195: `arbiter114_rate=0.35`  
  * B\_post195: `arbiter114_rate=0.50`  
  * S\_stress144: `arbiter114_rate=1.00`  
  * Source: `sol_phase311_16ao_block_compare.csv`.  
* 16AP (hard baseline history probe) shows those block differences collapse:  
  * A\_pre195, B\_post195, S\_stress144 all have `p_arb114 = 1.0`  
  * Source: `sol_phase311_16ap_block_compare.csv`, pivot `sol_phase311_16ao_vs_16ap_arbiter114_rates_pivot.csv`.

  * A: median delta at arbiter tick ≈ \-0.000485, mean ≈ \-0.005954  
  * B: median ≈ 0.000003  
  * S: median ≈ 0.051053  
  * Source: `sol_phase311_16ao_delta_at_arbiterTick.csv` (summarized from that table).

**Threshold ridge & hysteresis**

* 16AR shows an abrupt transition:  
  * multB=0.80: `arb114_peakBus_rate=0.075`  
  * multB=0.90: `arb114_peakBus_rate=0.975`  
  * and lane/winner coupling:  
    * domLane `114->126` → peakBus winner always 114 (250/250)  
    * domLane `136->7` → mixed winners (114:32, 136:38)  
  * Sources: `sol_phase311_16ar_rates_by_multB.csv`, `sol_phase311_16ar_lane_vs_winners_overall_peakBus.csv`.  
* 16AV current-baseline down sweep shows peak winner probability stays 0 until \~0.94 and becomes 1.0 by 0.96:  
  * multB=0.94: `P114_peak=0.1333`  
  * multB=0.96: `P114_peak=1.0`  
  * Source: `sol_phase311_16av_rates_by_multB.csv`.  
* 16AW dense microscope estimates:  
  * `p114_peak` threshold cross at **0.941957** (bracket 0.94–0.945)  
  * `p114_t8` threshold cross at **0.973947** (bracket 0.97–0.975)  
  * Source: `sol_phase311_16aw_threshold_estimates.csv`.  
* 16AY hysteresis (preconditioned) threshold estimates:  
  * peakBus threshold:  
    * **down:** 0.9525  
    * **up:** 0.9565  
    * hysteresis\_width ≈ 0.004  
  * tick8 threshold (up only in-range): 0.979375  
  * Turbulence index peaks around multB 0.955–0.965 (direction-dependent).  
  * Sources: `sol_phase311_16ay_threshold_estimates.csv`, `sol_phase311_16ay_turbulence_profile.csv`.  
* 16AZ late-boundary hysteresis zoom logistic fit:  
  * peakBus x50:  
    * down: 0.953244  
    * up: 0.953080  
    * hysteresis\_width ≈ \-0.000164 (magnitude 0.000164)  
  * Source: `sol_phase311_16az_threshold_logistic_peakBus.csv`.  
* 16BA early-lock hysteresis:  
  * tick8 threshold is identical up/down: x50=0.975000  
  * `p114_t8=0.5` across 0.975–1.0 for both directions (plateau behavior)  
  * Source: `sol_phase311_16ba_rates_by_dir_multB.csv`, `sol_phase311_16ba_thresholds_t8.csv`.

### **Interpretations (INTERPRETATION)**

* The “ridge” behaves like a **phase transition** in winner identity that can be read out via:  
  * peakBus winner probability (fast),  
  * tick8 winner probability (later/stricter),  
  * lane identity dominance (structural signature).  
* The system exhibits **protocol-dependent hysteresis**:  
  * noticeable in 16AY (and visible in 16AX\_v2),  
  * minimal in 16AZ (zoomed late-boundary),  
  * absent/neutralized in 16BA (early-lock plateau).  
* The 16AO→16AP contrast indicates that what looked like “history sensitivity” can be **eliminated** by a stricter baseline initialization protocol (hard reset), implying prior results can be confounded by incomplete state reset.

### **Hypotheses generated (HYPOTHESIS)**

* **H1:** damp≈20 opens a “rare channel” with early marker tick \~8 and packet class `136_then_114_fast`, potentially acting as a *gate* into ridge-adjacent regimes.  
  * Falsify by repeating anomaly hunt across damp 18–22 under strict baseline v1.5 restore \+ fixed dt; verify whether anomaly\_rate remains uniquely elevated at damp≈20.  
* **H2:** Hysteresis width scales with “dwell time” / sweep cadence and with which metric is used (peakBus vs tick8).  
  * Falsify by holding `multB` at bracket midpoints for longer durations and repeating up/down sweeps with identical dwell schedules.  
* **H3:** Lane identity (e.g., `114->126`, `114->89`, `136->7`) is a more stable classifier than “winner” alone and predicts winner with high accuracy inside some regimes.  
  * Falsify by finding multB bands where lane classifier and winner decouple (requires targeted search around turbulence peaks).

### **Speculation / Poetic (SPECULATION/POETIC)**

* “Rare channels opening” language remains metaphorical; treat it as a shorthand for **low-measure high-impact regimes** near the ridge until a mechanistic model is confirmed.
