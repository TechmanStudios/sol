Preface

RUNEWEAVING is an initiatory encounter of archetypal dimension, focused through the rune stones—the ancient Norse system of writing. To the ancients, runes were far more than a tool of communication; they served as a sacred oracle, divining the energies of the mind and integrating them with matter.

In RUNEWEAVING, the seeker is visited by the "Magii" or oracle, Lindane—a high initiate from the past—who weaves the code of the runes into a journey through time. As Lindane places her soul into the body of the runes, the life of spirit emerges.

Through a flowing turn of words designed to stir the meditative disciple, each rune is revealed within an ancient philosophy—one deeply connected to the earth and sea. For each runic letter in RUNEWEAVING, three presentations are given:

    THE LAW (the Vikings introduced the word "law" into the English language)
    THE SPIRIT
    THE RIDDLE, tying the weave of each rune together.

If the theory of relativity holds true, then the past, present, and future are not as separate as they seem. Perhaps we simply devise ways to interpret a single constant by breaking it down into manageable parts.

It may be that the mind itself acts as a barrier to our mutual coexistence with the entire spiral of awareness, space, and time. If this is the case, then runes could serve as a system of integration—one that, when reflected upon our inner spaces, reverses the entropy of the mind, re-creating the complete image of this spiral as a holograph upon each facet of the multifaceted soul.

I, Bathmenkin, Rune Master of the Vale, cast a runeweaving for thee.

In the breast of Catharri, the weaving begins—where the beauty of the sacred was gathered, and the seed engendered in love. The priestess Catharri, ripened in the embrace of Panthor, who stirred her womb to life, gave birth to Lindane—child of moon and stars, child of sun and sea, daughter of Panthor, the life host of the Heritage of One.

’Twas on the shores of Thrace that Lindane was born, where the Goddess silkens the mists and the sea foam lathers like milk from a doe, curling in traces of froth about the footstools of the eternal cliffs.

I came, in my eightieth sun-passing, to this great northern coast, seeking the child whose face shimmered in trancelight, mirrored in my crystal soul-compass. The ship that bore me to Thracean waters was a craft of the Fartherings—voyagers of the Great Sea, who guide their ships by the shining voices of the Heaven Speakers, sailing to shores beyond the known world.

The priestess Catharri was a Farthering, fostered as a child in Thrace by the Shamana Drea.

The far-distanced sound of harrow tone-laced the air. Harrow—birds of the sea, seldom seen—glided overhead, their gray-silver forms plumed in violet-banded pinions, they were like ghost ships haunting solitude. The harrow brought a thought-bonding to me with the violet-eyed child, Lindane. Her winged mind was forever in flight, the Sight her constant companion.

Truly, she was Magii, for no mere initiate could cast time as she could. Nor could any priestess alone blend minds with dolphin and honey bee alike. No—Lindane was Magii, as Catharri well knew.

At birth, Lindane had been tattooed by her priestess mother with the sign of the Spiritual Warrior—one who goes before the tide, cutting the sea like a ship’s bow; one who waits for time to move them, yet moves so that time may pass.

Thus began the testament of Magii Lindane, and through my Mastership, the child became the initiate—one who lived not in the letter of life but in the spirit of life. With every breath, she sifted the finer gold from knowledge, until she was adorned with wisdom and vested with the sweetness of the hidden heart.

As the runes speak the weaving of Life and Spirit, so did Lindane’s soul visit the body of the runes, breathing new life into their ancient testament. Upon these pages, the Magii’s passing among the runes is given as her hand set it down—by the writhe of a candle’s shifting light, as the twilight of an age sought to hold back the darkness for but a moment longer.

LINDANE SPEAKS ON THE HISTORY OF THE RUNES

Upon the Oath of the Sunstone, do I, Lindane, give pledge that what I score this night is a sacred passage—words possessed by the Gods of Truth and Wisdom.

It was the mighty Odin, crucified upon the branch of knowledge, who plunged his hand into the abyss and drew forth the runes of wisdom—insight beyond the Vale. It was Odin who inscribed the runes upon the first Priestess of Light, Andrea, from whom the Living Circle of the Sunstone was raised.

We who serve the Sunstone are the Living Circle, descendants of the first generation of Andrea, daughter of the Living Flame.

In an age distant, our ancestors became divided—like a tree whose limbs grew apart, thoughtless of the root that binds them as one. Many from the Tribe of Andrea sought the path of the Red Dragon—a path of renewal through blood-bonding, the taking of one strength to supply another. The path of the Red Dragon is one of surrender to the eternal cycles of birth and death.

The Red Dragon consumes its own flesh and fire, replenishing itself through living sacrifice, on the altar and in battle. It is the Valkyrie of dark spaces, feasting on revenge, blotting out suns in its path, tearing into the hearts of violent men.

Yet, there are those of Andrea's lineage who, like the Priestess of Light, walked the path of the Golden Dragon. It is from the mouth of the Golden Dragon that I speak, for it is he who guards the Supreme Sunstone. We of the Circle of the Sunstone continue our service to the Living Lights, as did our One Sent, Andrea, when her very body became a kenning of runic verse, scored by Odin in the shadow of the Sacred Tree.

Among us are Priest, Priestess, Shaman, Shamana, Druii (Master of the Vale), and Magii. Each title bears a sacred duty, given form in rune upon the body of Andrea:

    The Priest’s inscriptions lie upon her feet and legs.
    The Priestess’s runes are incised upon her hands, arms, and chest.
    The Shaman’s runes are graven upon her lips.
    The Shamana’s symbols adorn her neck.
    The Druii’s runes flow along her spine and stomach.
    And it is the Magii whose runic magic is scored upon Andrea’s heart and forehead, burning also from the crown of her head.

Together, they reflect the body of wisdom, the spirit of the Law of One, bestowed upon us by the One Sent from the fiery castles of the Living Lights.

And so the runes bid us to memory, to seed our tribe, ourselves, and our generation. Never shall the Circle wither around the Sunstone, so long as the runes sing to us.

1. THE SELF - Mannaz
THE LAW

Spirit made man consumes the restive heart. Fill the tidal pools with the vision of Mannaz, the rune of the Self—consummated with form, given earthly substance through order and self-rule.

For the initiate who lives this rune, self-rule is the first proclamation of true and infinite existence. He who knows his own worth knows the value of the universe, and the sum of the many human tokens of spirit we claim as souls.

In each soul, we find a vestige of the Earth-Soul. Like a member of Andrea’s body, so we inscribe our lives upon a token of spirit. It is the voice of Mannaz that speaks to us in the sea wind, murmured memories of the bond we hold to the earth, and the cycles of time that fill us with great and small things.

Each of us is alone with the Self. We must find completeness in that knowledge before we can know emancipation from the draft of time. The nets are cast in the sea womb for all of us, and to the end of the Sea-Life shall we inevitably be drawn.

The greater Self is our portion of immortality, the elixir that raises us to new life among the stars.

THE SPIRIT

I gather my Self unto me from the silver traces of the Sea Queen’s hair, washed in the scent of the moon—flowing, flooding, surging, breaking upon the shore.

As my life ebbs, the strength of my Self increases. Like a golden Dragonship, it prepares me for my journey. From the center of my being, the dragon stirs. Its flashing eyes both devour and sustain me.

Within myself, I am both the slain and the slayer.

Golden One, carry me beyond the Sea-Gate, where I may turn away the sword and embrace eternity.

THE RIDDLE

Dragons battle in the night,
One is Darkness, one is Light.
From their midst, a ship doth sail,
Past the barrier of the Vale.

2. PARTNERSHIP - Gebo
THE LAW

For the initiate of the runes, Gebo is the unifier—the bridge between matter and spirit, self with Self, land with sea, and time with space. From that which brings together, purpose arises.

From the first Magii, Andrea, we learn the importance of union, for she was transformed from mortal to immortal through her union with Odin. As he inscribed the Law upon her, she became the Sunstone, the Supreme Runestone, the Soul Compass of the Divine.

Yet once union is accomplished, it must give way to separation, only to be followed by reunion.

It is the semen of the stag (Retreat) ripened in the womb of the mare (Movement) that brings forth a new life force—the unicorn. Yet the unicorn must pierce the womb of its mother and challenge its sire to ensure the singularity of its form. Reunion is realized when aloneness is found to be the only reward of uniqueness.

And when souls co-mingle, separateness becomes more valued, for it is shared. To share is the quest of the human heart. The unicorn learns to seek union despite its deep need for solitude.

To find both union and solitude abiding together—this is the Law of this rune fulfilled.

THE SPIRIT

As sun-stars settle upon the shoulders of the Maiden Mountain, I am alone. All the empty spaces beckon me, calling me to follow the unicorn, where marbled stone gleams like dragon’s teeth in the halls of solitude.

The call of the Alone One is sweet and tender; it is sad, close to the lips of a parting psalm. I sing the sadness, yet my heart flees.

Oh Mother of the Mountain, grant me flight. Stretch out thy hand and give me comfort. May I find the balance within me, to know that I am not truly alone.

Yearning, I look into the pool of my own tears, and there I behold the face of my constant companion—the one who sustains me, the one who knows the secrets of my soul.

THE RIDDLE

Steed of time, bring us near
To the union we most fear.
For often do we not yet know
From what meeting may our blessings flow?

3. SIGNALS - Ansuz
THE LAW

Ansuz is the Law of Retrieval. Its animal form is the white owl, piercing the night with its beacon eyes—lanterns of wisdom for those whose sight cannot break the seal of darkness.

The Messenger God, Loki, travels in the breast of the white owl, moving from place to place, gathering the signs and symbols of lost knowledge.

There is no new truth, for time is a circle, where the answers of the Gods determine our questions. Thus, nothing of wisdom or folly is born anew; it must be retrieved from ancient and often abandoned conclusions.

The initiate is taught to remain ever watchful for the signals of life—the bent twig, the sudden storm, the trill of a black nightcatcher. He must not be lulled by the ordinary, for the truest mark is often struck by the wayward arrow.

When our One Sent came from Odin’s Tree in the Sacred Grove of the First Land and walked among the tribes of earth, she became Loki, the white owl. Like the owl, Andrea could not speak to impart her message. Like the owl, she could only wait for those who would see—to read the signals placed upon her by Odin, who had retrieved them from the abyss.

THE SPIRIT

Moon owl, watch me.
See my stillness before thee.
I listen with my eyes.
I seek the distance with my ears.

The scent of truth lingers in the cool air, as thy wings flutter—slapping muffled notes upon the harp of the wind.

Should I find thee before me, I would shut away my dwelling.
My sheep would be set into the hills.
My fields, left to the sun.
And I would follow thee.

For no manner of life is whole without thy inscriptions—thy verse of old.
From thy wisdom, I receive my spirit.
Whatever is lacking in me, abides in thee.

Thou art the intercessor for my soul.

THE RIDDLE

Claws of owls fasten my soul,
To myths and magic and truths untold.
Signal fires dance for me,
Of runes and muses that I might see.

4. RETREAT - Othila
THE LAW

In the Ancient Days, the First Generation walked the earth in vessels of light, their flesh the substance of amber, held to the flame. They knew no want, no pain, no death. They built no shelters, nor did they claim dominion over any being or thing.

But as the ages filled the cup of time, the People became like the stag, fleeing from the hunter’s spear. They had begun to covet pain, for it gave them knowledge of pleasure. They lusted after death, for in its shadow lay the secret of life.

They became infants of their own consequence, and their flesh grew heavy and soft, now feeling cold and heat. They cried out for the Goddess to comfort them, to spread her robes about them.

The Earth Mother replied,
"Who speaks my name?"

And the People moaned,
"Thy children!"

The Goddess answered,
"Nay, my children do not cast shadows, nor do they leave any trace upon the sand. They are the wind, the light of the sun, the scent of the flower. Ye are but orphaned thoughts that have trespassed upon the surface of still waters. Yet, I shall embrace thee, for I turn none away who calls my name.

I will bind thee to an oath: cast aside all that thou hast gathered, the ways thou hast taken unto the fold—those things that cling to thee and claim thee. Only then shall ye be received unto me."

Many among the People turned away, running back into the night, seeking their tools of dominion, kindling fires to shut out the blackness of their fate.

Only a few remained with the Goddess, setting aside all that was defiled in Her sight.

It is to those who follow the Goddess that the Law of Othila speaks.

Cycles demand release from old ways that have grown stagnant. To be true to this knowledge is to leave behind that which no longer serves your nature, in order to receive your true inheritance—that which is garnered through allegiance to the deepest truth within you.

THE SPIRIT

I came to the Old One, who sits in the cavern.

Upward did I climb to the great yawning mouth, where no light took root within.

"Old One," I pondered,
"Why do thee sit in darkness?"

The Old One rasped, in the voice of the cave wind,
"I hold the dark face to the earth so that others may know they are graced with light."

And so it is truth, that we see and know through the lesson of opposites.

We must be shown sorrow to know happiness.
We must be presented with evil to find the divine within us.

Thus, for those who retreat from the day, the truth of the light is given to them and through them.

THE RIDDLE

Stag of Silence, crowned in light,
Slip me past the hunter’s sight.
Give me strength to endure the night.

5. STRENGTH - Uruz
THE LAW

To receive the ritual of Uruz, the initiate must enter the place of darkness, where he neither sees nor hears, save for the heartbeat of silence.

Strength is found through inner conquest. To perceive the battle within, the initiate must renounce the world of stars and sun and descend into the depths of his own passions, where his senses are confined to the many-chambered soul.

Once the intimate duel of forces within has been met, the Spiritual Warrior returns to the World of Light, the rune of Strength emblazoned upon his soul-shield.

The Law of Uruz speaks to each of us:
We may only know our own strength through direct confrontation with our most entrenched fears.

Stripped of our otherworldly armor, we invite battle with ourselves.

When we return to the Light, we do so having relinquished some part of our nature, accepting the inevitable decay of life as the prophecy of new birth.

THE SPIRIT

In the belly of the giant, I am the waiting warrior, stalking the beast within.

I stand, wrapped in a well of impenetrable madness, seeking the sanity of light, the sound of life echoing through distant voids.

I reach for the dark crimson robe that had caressed my shoulder but a moment before—only to find that even that vestige has been stripped away.

Colors swim in my mind as I reach into the fading rainbow for the crimson warmth I seek. Yet I know I will soon dispatch my wandering to silent fields—

Colorless. Dawnless.
Skies drenched in nothingness, spilling over into Odin’s abyss.

Time becomes timeless, and I dwell in the depth of loss, marooned like a death ship upon a shoreless sea, awaiting the pyre to carry me into eternity.

Then, from the depths of my being, there emerges a surge of life—a restored mastery so intense it breaks golden upon the greyness of my shore.

Suddenly, I am alight from within.
My ship is set free.
The old is burned away.
The warrior is washed clean in the flames.

My soul rises from the pyre like a dove, seeking new and tender greenness.

My mind burns crimson.
All colors flood me, carrying me onward,
Upward—like a wave of perfect strength and grace.

And when I step once more into the lighted world, the sights and sounds shall hold for me a new purpose.

THE RIDDLE

Ship of death upon the fire,
Burnished flames become a lyre.
String my soul upon the stars,
Send my spirit to worlds afar.

6. INITIANTION - Perth
THE LAW

Once more, the initiate must know the flame to find the truth of Perth—the rune that succeeds Uruz.

The dove is the spirit-animal of Strength, for the greatest strength has been purified into gentleness, lightness, and perfect peace.

Yet, as this perfection rises from the fire, another spirit-elemental is also born—the power of hidden knowledge, which must endure all attempts to violate its truth.

The spirit eagle is bound to the mystery of transformation, where knowledge becomes truth. Lifted into the graces of the sky, Perth is that which perceives all, yet judges not.

It is the veiled part of the Self, concealed from ignorance, for it must withhold divine knowledge from the uninitiated, lest they be destroyed by the strength of Thor’s hammer.

Only he who can embrace the dove may behold the eagle.

THE SPIRIT

The fires form circles about my soul.

I am an ember of a once-living tree, long suckled by the earth, now felled and shrunken by heat, a glowing breath of last light.

Yet, as my wholeness is consumed, I am made complete in another form.

I have become the eagle, knowing, yet unknown by mortal ones.

In flight, I turn to the sanctuary of the winds, for there my secret is kept, in clouded repose.

I was once rooted to a quaking earth, braced against the storm, sheltering the fawn.

Now, I am enlightened to the world of the sky, where I may see all that has come before and all that is yet to be.

Where once I was seed, now I am sunlight.

THE RIDDLE

Revealed truth from hidden halls,
Phoenix-graced, the ashes fall.
Tower of Light doth arise for thee,
From the root of the Sacred Tree.

7. CONSRAINT - Nauthiz
THE LAW

The fish of sorrow is Nauthiz—the sign of limitations, brought forth by the soul’s need to restore balance among the many-turned faces of the dragon.

The spirit cannot be contained, but the soul is like the little fish, ever wary of the hidden places, the dark recesses within its world, where survival demands caution.

So must we regard our enemies of self—as warriors, with whom we often do battle, yet must always hold in respect.

The Law of Constraint is submerged within the final lesson of Strength, for both proclaim that Light survives even the darkest hour.

With Strength, the initiate submits willingly to the testing of this truth.
With Constraint, he is bowed by the force of it.

Where he once failed to grasp this principle through his own volition, he is now charged with compliance—by Divine Will.

THE SPIRIT

I am the minnow, composed in light.
I am the pattern of my universe, the shadow, the dark,
The webbed fingers of light that silkens my breathing world.

Without these rituals of light and dark, I am without substance,
For I am the dimension of that which identifies me.

Thus, as the initiate, I must come to know myself as part of that which contains me.

The ocean is no more than the drops of water it holds,
Yet within those drops, the whole world is mirrored.

THE RIDDLE

Circle of Dark, Circle of Light,
My soul is the flame from which both take their right.

8. FERTILITY - Inguz
THE LAW

From the terraces of the Living Lights, this rune is given. It is inscribed in the starry wonder, that we may one day be emancipated from the gravity of our lives—through some working of the divine, on a day un-numbered yet awaiting us, a future promise.

Our pursuit of one another, as companions of the Path, is a yearning deep within us. It is our Swan Song, lamenting the absence of oneness.

Like the swan, we must wait for our process to mature—for the moment when we shall be received in new birth. This change is not immediate, but deliberate and complete.

The Law of Inguz whispers to the initiate’s spirit, telling him that the beauty of the full-grown swan already dwells within.

It is for the seeker to progress within the mold of this insight, to shape himself until his form reflects his true beauty.

And when the initiate is received in his true raiment, he is joined with the bride of eternity.

THE SPIRIT

As the chalice of the Moon Goddess spills upon still waters,
I long to drink from its sweetness.

The mist, perfumed in honey scent,
Cress and paling lily,
Awakens my long-sleeping heart.

I am supine, fluid as the liquid light.
I am no longer the child of consequence,
But feel myself grow and bend,
Winnowing out the marrow of my struggle
With pinions of snow.

Grace becomes my master,
And I lift from my grassy nest,
Journeying through rose-gathered places
And sapphire lakes,
Where I become an expression of the first sunrise.

THE RIDDLE

Masked is the beauty of those who mourn,
For what they cannot see.
Adorned in beauty is the one,
Who knows that what will be, will be.

9. DEFENSE - Wihwaz
THE LAW

Eihwaz is joined to Inguz, blazing upon the runic field—a heraldry of the Lion of Defense and the Swan of Fertility, together forging the path of the Spiritual Warrior.

Inguz, the force of completion, is guarded and tempered by the avertive powers of Eihwaz.

The lion walks through the forest, leaving no mark, no sound of his passing. His eyes seek the surest path.

So must the initiate come to command the beast of his nature, setting it as guardian over his discrimination, patience, and perseverance.

When the first Magii, Andrea, went forth from the Sacred Grove, the great golden lion was her escort, and she summoned the runic power of Eihwaz to guide her beyond the barriers of the Old Seals—those forces of the Before Time that had imprisoned the earth in ignorance.

THE SPIRIT

Rise up and go before me,
Guardian of my temple, keeper of my flame.

Lion of the Sun, thou art the sword in the forest,
Waiting to be grasped by my hand.

Combine with me,
And let us become the seasons of my passing upon the earth.

With thy rune of power upon me,
I shall know each season in its time.

Eihwaz, encircle me,
Thy patient voice speaks the language of endurance.

I have entered the Cities of the Dead,
Where only the raven perches upon idols with lips of stone.

There, I have seen the dissolution of evil,
The empty halls and vacant eyes fastened to darkness.

I have passed beyond the mountain, into the valley,
Where long grasses sweep an azure sky,
And cherry birds are rampant in fields of wild rice.

I have known the fullness of nature’s harvest,
And seen the children of the Goddess emerge in silver shadows,
Tender creatures from Her bosom,
Standing at the brink of waters,
Lavender-streaked by the brush of twilight.

All the secrets of the night are told to me,
In the communion of evening.

As the initiate, I know I stand at the parting of the Two Ways.

I become a command spoken by Spirit.
I am the word that decides the journey.

THE RIDDLE

Old worlds die, new ones rise,
I see them all in the lion’s eyes.
He leads me through the way revealed,
Past those whose sight is forever sealed.

10. Protection - Algiz
THE LAW

The safe harbor is Algiz—the rune of perspective, from which the mystic soldier surveys the terrain and charts his course.

The initiate aligns his focus between the horns of the elk, arched in perfect balance with the space between.

A steady grip decides the warrior’s aim.
The motionless thought charges the soul to action.

He who advances before the call is given spills his life upon the field of battle.

For the warrior of spirit, the battle is the self with the Self.

Unto each age, there is a service to perform.
Unto each initiate of the age, a duty is ordained.

The Law of Protection is thus:

Whoever meets the challenge of his soul with forethought and control of emotion is granted the protection of Spirit, for he fulfills that which has been entrusted unto him in the name of the fold.

No initiate serves for himself alone,
But also for his comrades-at-arms, who support him in battle,
And for the faint of heart, who cannot yet lift the sword.

THE SPIRIT

The retreat of summer plucks at the trees,
Emptying branches, filling meadows
With the tokens of the Goddess—
Golden, crimson, pale moonlight—
Each leaf a history of a tribe among the forest of trees.

The black oak, apple, and elm
Stand as living testaments of the earth.

From among the bared teeth of approaching winter,
The elk lifts his head.
He halts his feeding to survey the land.
He sees what passes, he knows what is to come.

Like the elk, there is no wrenching in my heart
For the day of summer,
Nor foreboding at the glimpse of first snow.

As one who stands initiated before the throne of the Goddess,
I am at peace—
With the resolution of spaces,
The capture of time in the nets of the stars,
And the judgment of the Gods beyond the threshold.

THE RIDDLE

Amidst the world, I am at peace,
With all those things which never cease.
I stand apart, yet still abide,
With rumbling earth and ocean tide.

11. POSSESSIONS - Fehu
THE LAW

The time has come to receive blessings.

The initiate has weathered the storm, held fast to the torch of Spirit, and proclaimed his worth in both word and deed.

Thus, he has reached a plateau of prosperity—of both spiritual and, often, material gain.

The Law of Fehu is a radiance between the mind and heart.

There must be a sacred exchange between these two centers within the initiate,
For only then may he receive the fullness of this rune.

Fehu is like the fox,
Who swiftly and silently gathers the best elements of the physical world,
Preparing his den for winter’s ravage.

Yet, to the People of the North, the fox is not merely clever,
But also rich in spirit,
For he frolics in the midst of hardship
And knows that life is to be enjoyed.

We who dwell in rapport with the creatures of the sea and the field
Hold this small, darting woods-fellow in reverence.

Like the fox, servants of the Goddess must strive to fill the soul before the body,
For the soul is the fountainhead,
And the body is the pool below.

THE SPIRIT
Where the land was bare, now it rejoices.
The sun beats the snow to fire-gold.

I follow the winter fox past the drifts of time
To the Circle of Stone.

Here, faeries dance,
Their shadows like puppets whirling upon the ground.

There is warmth within the circle.
Life clings close to the vine.

Yet beyond this faerie’s lair,
The wind thrusts its blade into the trees,
Cutting troughs through the frozen ground.

I sit at the center of the circle,
Near the Yehti—the fire that bears no flame,
A heat engendered through magic.

I am robed in the white skin of the winter fox,
Marked with black-tipped spirit signs,
From woods-fellows who have died a bloodless death.

Their treasured fur veils my head,
Their snow-warmth mittens my hands.

Another form steps into the circle.
She stands at the rim of the light,
Waiting to be called inside.

Her unbound hair, shimmering cornsilk,
Is her only shield against the starkness of a December night.

It is my place to summon her.

At my command, her body touches the light,
And is enveloped by it.

As holy bread, her naked flesh is sopped in golden wine.

I disrobe, letting the soft skins of the fox fall from my shoulders.
For a moment, the wind mocks me, and I am afraid.
Yet I do not falter.

I take up the furs and place them upon her.

Her face is young,
But in the light of the Yehti,
Her eyes speak the wisdom of a Shamana,
Whose thoughts are harvested from the fields of the Goddess.

Though I have given the sacred robes to her living stream,
And now go forth into the unforgiving night of a dark moon,

I am warmed and full with life,
My worth carried within me—
A bounty of the heart.

THE RIDDLE
Tithers leave beside the way,
Bits of life with which to pay.
But none is worth the gift that’s given,
From the life that’s worth the living.

12. JOY - Wunjo

THE LAW
Once the testing of Fehu is met, the initiate is bathed in the Light of Joy,
For he has met the sunrise at the crossroads of the summer sky
And caught the rainbow as it showered down upon the land.

He has become the warrior in his moment of victory.

The Red Dragon burns golden for him,
And he is helmeted with wings of valor.

The Law of Joy is this:
To experience it fully,
To invest ourselves in the radiance of the moment,
And then to send it forth, love-embraced,
Out upon the skies, to another soul in need of succor.

In peacock splendor, the initiate captures the essence of Wunjo,
Transmuting evil to good,
Knowledge to wisdom,
Sorrow to joy.

Restored and reinvigorated,
He who is gifted with the rune of Joy
Is displayed as a host to the sun.

THE SPIRIT
Wunjo, patron of happiness,
Send thy song to me.

I am the peacock soul,
Adorned in the colors of the emerald sea and indigo sky.

As thy seeker, I stretch out my being before thy font.
I am received,
Arisen from my travail,
As one who lays aside the sword to lift the cup.

Victory is my shield,
For I have vanquished the missiles of sorrow that plummeted me,
And gathered unto my spirit
A many-splendored reverie.

LINDANE'S JOURNEY TO THE STONE OF KEL:
Questing, did I, Lindane, come unto the Isle of the Celts, to the Sacred Stone of Kel, drawn by the whisper of fate and the silent call of the ancient runes. Astride my mount, Golandor, I forged across mountain streams, the cold bite of the water cleansing my path, and I descended into the vale, where foxes hunted quail in sheltering forests, and fog-draped castles lurched from the shadowed crags of forgotten places. My journey led me beyond the ruins of time, past broken stones, monuments once standing in solemn grandeur, now fallen, their empty faces staring skyward, their whispers carried away by the winds of history.

Through the golden hush of twilight, I came upon a meadow, its grasses ruddy-gold beneath a harvest moon, luminous and trembling in the breath of the night. From the center of the plain, an ancient mound rose, round as the womb of the Goddess, full in the moment before birth. At its crest stood the Stone of Kel, unmoving in its celestial flight, its form etched in eternity, untouched by the shifting tides of mortal hands.

It was the Law-Speaker of the runes, struck by Odin’s hand on a mid-summer’s night, when the world lay at the breast of the Mother, when light-halos wrapped the children of the earth in swaddling cloths of mist and memory.

Before the Stone, I stood, my cloak of white owl feathers ruffled by the night breezes, carrying forth the mystery of the dark wood, weaving shadow and moonlight into the silent hymn of the meadow. The symbols carved upon the Stone—runes of strength, power, and beauty—burned with the fire of the past, and like ancient arrows, they found my mind a willing target, piercing through the veils of time and striking my soul with the wisdom of the ages.

It was a portal to visions, a threshold beyond sight, and before its power, the Vale was lifted from my eyes.

Upon Odin’s tongue, I beheld the Sacred Tree, rising beyond the waking world, its crown of leaves filtering the first light of creation, its limbs and trunk bathed in golden rays, scattering like shafts of wheat upon the thrashing bed of time. There, stretched upon its boughs, was a man, his arms outstretched in a gesture of flight from bondage, suspended between the earth and the heavens, neither forsaken nor free.

Gentle was his form, graceful in supplication, his lifted brow constant in reverent fold, his eyes sunlit seas, deep and warm, holding within them the hidden treasures of the soul. His hair, the stain of mahogany, fell in solemn strands over hollowed cheeks, and his face, smooth and worn as tallow melted in flame, bore the weight of time and suffering, softened only by the gravity of divine grace.

His mouth was sorrow, pain unraveled from longing, resting within the shelter of unseen hands, bearing the weight of ages in a single breath. And in that moment, as I stood transfixed, the vision shook the marrow of my being, and I knew—I had gazed upon the Sun-King, crucified upon the Tree of Life, woven into the roots of existence itself.

As the first rose-petals of dawn scattered upon the sky, I departed the Meadow of Kel, my hands empty, yet my bosom filled with the vision, a repast for my soul, the truth sought and found within the kennings of my heart.

At the woods’ edge, where the faries’ mist curled over the earth, Golandor stood waiting, trembling beneath the hush of morning’s breath. I rode from that sacred place, my heart scored with the runes of Kel, knowing I would never return, yet knowing too that my soul would forever wander there.

For when the harrow crests the sea, and the clouds form pictures in my mind, the Sight awakens within me once more, and in the depths of sunrise, the Sun-King rides across the heavens, flinging runes of fire upon the dawn, whispering eternity to the waiting sky.

GLOSSARY

    VALE – A name given by the Living Circle of the Sunstone to the barrier between the mortal and immortal worlds, the veil separating earthly existence from the higher realms of spirit.

    FARTHERINGS – A name referring to the Vikings, those who voyaged across the great seas, following the whispers of the stars and the pull of destiny.

    HEAVEN SPEAKERS – The heavenly bodies of the stars, regarded in their aspect of imparting knowledge, whether it be astronomical, astrological, or oracular. The stars, as silent sentinels of time, hold within them the wisdom of the cosmos.

    SUNSTONE – A homogeneity of spiritual law, focused into a crystalline energy structure, acting as a tribal frequency that influences the genetic and para-genetic coding of a race or people. Historically, a sunstone or sólarsteinn was a translucent crystal of calcite (Iceland spar), used by the Vikings as a navigational compass, as it could polarize light and determine the position of the sun even in overcast weather. In the mystic tradition, the Sunstone is both a physical tool and a metaphysical conduit, linking those who wield it to the higher currents of existence.

    VALKYRIE – In Viking literature, a female ghost-form haunting the battlefield, seeking blood revenge and guiding the souls of warriors to their fated end. She is both a harbinger of death and a guardian of the chosen, standing at the threshold between life and the halls of the fallen.

    KENNINGS – Elaborate runic images, used in a complex form of Viking verse known as Skaldic poetry. These metaphoric expressions weave layers of meaning into words, binding language with mysticism, history, and the unseen forces of fate.

    LIVING LIGHTS – The Hierarchy of Light Beings, residing within the energy of the stars, governing and guiding the world. They are the flames of creation, the watchers of destiny, and the silent weavers of the Great Tapestry.

    FUTHARK – The original runic alphabet, the sacred script inscribed upon the fabric of time, a language not merely of words, but of power, prophecy, and divine law.
